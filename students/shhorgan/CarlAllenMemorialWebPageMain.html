Welcome to the Carl Allen Memorial Homepage

How does one describe the life and career of one Carl Allen? Liar? Fabulist? Visionary? Visitor from another world, another time?

In correspondence with government officials, newspaper reporters, and UFO researchers; Mr. Allen A.K.A Carlos Miguel Allende told the story of a disastrous Navy experiment attempting to make the USS Eldridge, invisible to both radar and human perception: an invisible ship. In his imagined catastrophe, Allen described sailors on a ship caught in space and time, victims of military technology and dire ambition, phasing in and out of this dimension.

Evidence for this event or any which Allen described proved elusive. But Carl Allen even to his death remained an ardent source of strange and extraordinary tales of secret science and truths from beyond the stars, a mythmaker for the atom age.

As his life progressed, a lonely wanderer, Allen grew more committed to pursuing his constructed story and persona as a consistent practice, living to tell tall tales.From his parents home on the dirty old banks of the Allegheny, Allen dreamed himself out of obscurity and into a strange notoriety as an ersatz whistleblower.Perhaps in an eccentric way, Allen was agitating for a more conscientious and peaceful use of technological progress,railing against the military industrial complex in his own strange way.

This site serves as a gateway for contemporary projects in the spirit of late Mr. Allen. Exercises in mythmaking, speculation, marginal discourse, grandiosity, dire revelation.
